Emotional Double Standard:-
    Expecting emotional sensitivity from others, but responds to others with logical instead of empathy.

    Egocentric emotional processing:-
        He interrupts emotions mainly from his perspective, not the other person
        Lacking empathy.
        Emotionally self-centered but not intentionally selfish

Envious:- 
    The person who gets jealous of recognition his friend received.
    He wants the same recognition, praise or attention his friend is getting and feels disturbed
    when he doesn't receive it.

    Envy involves wishing you had what another person has, jealosuly.

Competitive Insecurity:- 
    Even though its a friendship, he internally treats its like comparision scale
    He wants to be better than his friend in some aspect and feels insecure when he is not.
        "Why him, not me ???"
        "I should be the one noticed."

Inferiority-triggeres jealousy:-
    The friends success or praise activates his own insecurity. "Maybe Im Less"

Image Management / Reputation Saving:- 
    Even though he feels envy, jealosuly, he wont express to save his status.
    He just wont go near that topic which triggered his Inferiority/Insecurity. Avoids it

Covert Social Manipulation driven by personal dislike:-
    He wants others to adopt his negative view od someone, but he does it indirectly to protect
    his image.
    Covert means hidden, secretly or dishuised referring to actions or things not openly practiced
    or shown. Often used in military contexts

Triangulation:- 
    Instead of resolving his issues with that person, he tries to influence others against them.

Ambivalence:- 
    Holding strong positive and negative feelings simultaneously toward the same person.

Ambivalent attachment:- 
    Common in close relationships where a person:
        craves closeness
        Is easily hurt by the same person
        Becomes resentful beacuse the other person matters so much

Low Emotional Expressiveness:-
    He may feel care, affetcion or concern internally but doesn't know how to express them 
    behaviourlly.

    Low Empathic communication skill:-
        He doesn't know how to:-
            Consoling
            Reassuring
            Showing warmth
            Verbelly expressing care
        So when situations demand softness, he has no practiced response

Defensive Sarcasm:-
    Sarcasm and mild insults become a safe communication tool because they:-
        Don't expose vulnerability 
        Maintain emotional distance
        Feel socially easier than tendernes

Emotional Inhibition:-
    Positive emotions are suppressed or blocked in expression, often due to:-
        Upbringing
        Habit
        Discomfort with vulnerability
        Fear of appearing weak

Emotional rigidity:-
    The pattern is known as emotional rigidity with a single dominant soping style
    He relies on one emotional expression (sarcasm) across situations that actually require very 
    different emotional response.

    Inability to shift emotional tone based on context:-
        Sad moment -> Sarcasm
        Caring moment -> Sarcasm
        Serious talk -> Sarcasm
        Vulnerable moment -> Sarcasm
    He lacks emotional flexibility. He has emotional rigidity and uses sarcasm as a universal
    emotional mask
