    primary_fear:
    biggest_insecurity:
    core_wound:      # Past event that shaped them

An Example:-

        Step 1: Take a Filled Character (Example)

        I’ll use a short version of a filled template:
            name: Aarav

            primary_desire: To be respected and seen as powerful
            internal_need: To feel worthy without proving himself

            core_wound: Grew up with a father who constantly called him weak

            worldview: "Only power earns respect"
            belief_about_self: "I am nothing unless I dominate"

            values: control, respect, self-reliance
            non_negotiable: never appear weak in front of others

            breaking_point: humiliation in public

            personality:
            dominant: controlled, sharp, observant
            hidden: insecure, approval-seeking

            with_love_interest: distant, avoids vulnerability
            with_authority: competitive, subtly rebellious

            speech_pattern: short sentences, rarely explains himself
            body_language: still, intense eye contact

            contradiction: confident outside, fragile inside
====================================================================================
        Step 2: Convert Traits → Behavior Rules

            Now translate this into playable rules for scenes:

            “Never appear weak” → avoids apologizing, deflects emotion
            “Power = respect” → tries to control conversations
            Insecurity → overreacts to disrespect
            With love → pulls away when things get emotional

            👉 These become your behavior engine
====================================================================================
        Step 3: Build a Scene Using Conflict

            A scene =
            Goal + Obstacle + Behavior + Subtext

            SCENE EXAMPLE
            Context:

            Aarav’s girlfriend confronts him for being emotionally distant.

            SCREENPLAY STYLE (4 LAYERS)
            1. ACTION
            INT. CAR – NIGHT

            Rain hits the windshield. The engine is off.

            Aarav sits still, hands on the steering wheel.

            Maya turns toward him, frustrated.
            2. BEHAVIOR (What he does, not what he feels)
            Aarav doesn’t look at her.

            He tightens his grip on the wheel.

            When she speaks, he gives short responses.

            He checks his phone mid-conversation.

            👉 This shows:

            avoidance
            control
            emotional suppression
            3. DIALOGUE
            MAYA
            Why do you shut me out every time?

            AARAV
            I’m not shutting you out.

            MAYA
            You don’t talk. You don’t feel. What is this?

            AARAV
            This is me.
            4. SUBTEXT (What’s really happening underneath)
            She’s asking: “Do you care about me?”
            He hears: “You are weak and not enough”

            👉 So he defends, not connects.

            Key Moment (Character Revealed)
            MAYA
            I just want you to be honest.

            AARAV
            (looks at her, cold)
            And say what? That I need you?

            👉 That line = his wound speaking.

            Step 4: Add a Trigger → Escalation

            Now push his breaking point (humiliation)

            MAYA
            Maybe my friends were right about you.

            Aarav turns.

            That hits.

            Silence.

            AARAV
            What did they say?

            MAYA
            That you act strong because you’re scared.

            👉 Now:

            His wound is activated
            His belief is challenged
            Step 5: Show Choice (This defines character)

            Two options:

            Be vulnerable (growth)
            Assert control (current self)

            He chooses control:

            AARAV
            (smiles slightly)
            Then maybe you should listen to them.

            He starts the car.

            👉 He pushes her away instead of risking vulnerability.
====================================================================================
        Step 6: Extract the Pattern (So you can repeat)

            From this one character, you can generate endless scenes:

            Formula:
            Put them in a situation that challenges their belief
            Trigger their wound
            Force a choice
            Show behavior, not explanation
            Step 7: Quick Conversion Cheat Sheet

            When writing scenes, always translate like this:

            Template Element	Scene Translation
            Desire	What they try to get in the scene
            Fear	What they avoid saying/doing
            Wound	What triggers emotional reaction
            Belief	How they justify actions
            Trait	Body language + dialogue style
            Moral line	The decision moment
            Step 8: Mini Exercise for You

            Take your own character and write:

            A scene where someone challenges their belief
            Add a line that hits their wound
            Force them to choose:
            Stay who they are
            Or change
